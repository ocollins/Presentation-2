Beyond jQuery

    The good:

  • JavaScript Frameworks like jQuery help you out by abstracting hard and complex code.
  • Solid support for cross-browser issues.
  • Increased speed to market.
  • Wide range of plugins available for various specific needs.
  • AJAX support.
  • Great documentation and tutorials.

    The "not so good" and why solid understanding of Vanilla JavaScript is important:

  • jQuery has limitations where in more complex projects JS will be still needed.
  • jQuery bugs and performance issues (hide() and show()).
  • Hard to keep up with new JS Frameworks. More than 10 new JS Frameworks made the news in the past 5 years.
  • Solid understanding of Vanilla JavaScript will help to figure out problems with your JS Framework.
  • Employers are looking for good knowledge of JS.
